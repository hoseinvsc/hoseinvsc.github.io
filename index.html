About Gheymeh

In Iran, one of the most popular dishes is Gheymeh. It’s a stew made with split peas, pieces of lamb or beef, tomato sauce, and spices. What makes Gheymeh special is that it’s topped with crispy potato fries, which add a nice crunch. For many Iranians, Gheymeh is a comfort food that brings back memories of family gatherings and special events. It’s not hard to make, but it does take time to cook so the flavors can come together perfectly. We usually eat it with rice, and it’s delicious!

How to Make It:

  1. Cook the Meat and Peas: Fry chopped onions until golden, add small pieces of meat, turmeric, salt, and pepper. Then, add yellow split peas and cook together for a few minutes.
  2. Make the Sauce: Add tomato paste and some water to create a thick sauce. Simmer for about an hour until the meat and peas are soft.
  3. Add Dried Limes: Add dried limes (limoo amani) for a slightly sour taste, giving the stew its unique flavor.
  4. Prepare the Fries: Cut a potato into thin strips and fry until crispy. These fries go on top of the Gheymeh when ready.
  5. Serve: Serve the Gheymeh over rice and top with the crispy fries. Enjoy!

To me, Gheymeh is a perfect dish because it has a mix of flavors and textures. The soft stew with the crunchy fries on top is just so satisfying. It’s a meal that’s loved by families in Iran and brings people together. I always feel proud to share it with others because it’s part of our culture.
